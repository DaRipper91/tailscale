# Gemini Skill Manual: literature-review
## Format: 8-5-4 Standard

### Chapter 1: Introduction to Systematic Reviews
**1.1 Objective**
Master the art of synthesizing academic papers, identifying research gaps, and tracing the evolution of ideas.

**1.2 The Documentarian Mindset**
Treat every paper as a data point. Look for consensus, contradiction, and experimental rigor.

**1.3 The PRISMA Standard**
Follow the Preferred Reporting Items for Systematic Reviews and Meta-Analyses.

**1.4 Search Strategy**
Use `web-search-advanced-research-paper` to target arXiv, PubMed, and Google Scholar.

**1.5 Pro-Tip**
Use this skill to become an "instant expert" on a new scientific or technical field.

---

### Chapter 2: Defining the Research Question
**2.1 Objective**
Formulate a clear, focused, and answerable research question using the PICO framework.

**2.2 PICO Framework**
*   **P**opulation/Problem. **I**ntervention. **C**omparison. **O**utcome.

**2.3 Scope Definition**
Set boundaries on what papers will be included (e.g., "Only peer-reviewed," "Post-2020").

**2.4 Preliminary Search**
Perform a quick scan to ensure there is enough existing literature to support the review.

**2.5 Expected Outcome**
A "Protocol" document that guides the entire review process to prevent bias.

---

### Chapter 3: Advanced Literature Searching
**3.1 Objective**
Locate every relevant paper using systematic keyword and citation mapping.

**3.2 Keyword Expansion**
Find synonyms and technical terms used by different research groups for the same topic.

**3.3 Citation Snowballing**
Check the "References" of a good paper (Backward) and who "Cited" it (Forward).

**3.4 Managing Results**
Keep a running list of "Included" and "Excluded" papers with reasons for each.

**3.5 Pro-Tip**
Use Exa's `text` filter to find specific results that *must* contain a certain mathematical or technical term.

---

### Chapter 4: Data Extraction & Quality Appraisal
**4.1 Objective**
Extract relevant data from papers and evaluate their methodological quality.

**4.2 The Extraction Table**
Capture: Author, Year, Sample Size, Methodology, Key Findings, and Limitations.

**4.3 Risk of Bias (RoB) Assessment**
Identify potential flaws: Small sample sizes, funding conflicts, or lack of controls.

**4.4 Comparing Results**
Look for "Effect Sizes" or "Statistically Significant" differences across studies.

**4.5 Expected Outcome**
A "Cleaned" dataset representing the best available evidence on the topic.

---

### Chapter 5: Identifying Research Gaps
**4.1 Objective**
Find what is *not* being talked about or what the current research has failed to solve.

**5.2 Theoretical Gaps**
Cases where the existing models cannot explain the observed data.

**5.3 Methodological Gaps**
Opportunities to use newer or better tools to re-examine old problems.

**5.4 Conflicts in Literature**
Identify areas where Study A and Study B directly contradict each other.

**5.5 Pro-Tip**
Research gaps are "Business Opportunities." If you find a gap, you've found a place to innovate.

---

### Chapter 6: Synthesis & Narrative
**6.1 Objective**
Merge findings from dozens of studies into a coherent, evidence-based narrative.

**6.2 The Thematic Approach**
Group papers by the "Themes" or "Arguments" they support rather than by author.

**6.3 Tracing the Evolution**
Explain how the thinking on this topic has changed over time (Timeline of Ideas).

**6.4 Resolving Contradictions**
Explain *why* different studies might have reached different conclusions.

**6.5 Expected Outcome**
A comprehensive report that summarizes the "Current State of Knowledge" on the topic.

---

### Chapter 7: Ethics & Evidence Standards
**7.1 Objective**
Ensure your review is objective, transparent, and ethically sound.

**7.2 Avoiding Confirmation Bias**
Actively search for "Null Results" and papers that challenge your own assumptions.

**7.3 Data Privacy**
Ensure that no sensitive or private participant data from the original studies is leaked.

**7.4 Citing Everything**
Every claim MUST be linked back to a specific paper and section number.

**7.5 Pro-Tip**
Use the `peer-review` skill to critique your final report before publishing it.

---

### Chapter 8: Command Reference & FAQ
**8.1 Objective**
Quick lookup for academic research tools.

**8.2 Core Tools**
*   `web_search_advanced_research_paper`, `research_start`, `critical-analysis`.

**8.3 FAQ: Can it read paywalled papers?**
It can often find preprints (arXiv) or summaries. For full PDFs, you may need to provide them.

**8.4 FAQ: Is this only for science?**
No. Use it for Engineering, Law, History, or any field with a body of written research.

**8.5 Final Pro-Tip**
A literature review is the "Superpower" of a lifelong learner. Use it to stay ahead of your industry.
