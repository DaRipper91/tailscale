# Gemini Skill Manual: people-research
## Format: 8-5-4 Standard

### Chapter 1: Introduction to Professional Intelligence
**1.1 Objective**
Master the ability to find professional backgrounds, expertise, and public personas for any individual.

**1.2 The Ethical Boundary**
Focus on *public* and *professional* data. Respect privacy and avoid illegal doxxing.

**1.3 Discovery Workflow**
General Search -> Professional Profiles -> Contributions/News -> Synthesis.

**1.4 Tool Integration**
Primary use of `people_search_exa`, `web_search_advanced_personal_site`, and `web_search_advanced_tweet`.

**1.5 Pro-Tip**
Use this skill to research a hiring manager before an interview or an expert before a consultation.

---

### Chapter 2: Mapping Professional Profiles
**2.1 Objective**
Identify the current role, career history, and education of an individual.

**2.2 LinkedIn & Resumes**
Use `people_search_exa` to find LinkedIn profiles and professional CVs.

**2.3 Career Progression**
Analyze the "Timeline" of their career. Are they a generalist or a deep specialist?

**2.4 Education & Credentials**
Verify degrees, certifications, and specialized training mentioned in their bios.

**2.5 Expected Outcome**
A "Professional Snapshot" that summarizes their career path and current authority.

---

### Chapter 3: Contributions & Expertise
**3.1 Objective**
Determine what the person has actually *done* or *built*.

**3.2 Technical Contributions**
Search GitHub or Stack Overflow to see their code contributions and community help.

**3.3 Publications & Patents**
Find research papers, articles, or patents they have authored.

**3.4 Speaking & Media**
Identify podcasts, conference talks, or interviews where they share their expertise.

**3.5 Pro-Tip**
Search for their username across different developer platforms to find "hidden" expertise.

---

### Chapter 4: Public Persona & Opinions
**4.1 Objective**
Understand their communication style, public opinions, and "Vibe."

**4.2 Social Media Footprint**
Search Twitter/X and technical blogs to see what they are currently talking about.

**4.3 Public Sentiment**
How are they perceived by their peers? Are they cited or recommended by others?

**4.4 Key Themes**
Identify the "Big Ideas" or specific technologies they consistently advocate for.

**4.5 Expected Outcome**
A "Communication Profile" that helps you tailor your interactions with them.

---

### Chapter 5: Identifying Hidden Expertise
**5.1 Objective**
Find the specific "Niche" where this person truly excels.

**5.2 Community Recognition**
Look for "Stars," "Awards," or "Most Valuable Contributor" status on technical forums.

**5.3 Project Involvement**
Which high-impact projects have they been a part of? What was their specific role?

**5.4 Mentorship & Teaching**
Do they teach courses or mentor others? (Signals a deep, foundational understanding).

**5.5 Pro-Tip**
Use this to find "The Person" to ask when you have a specific, impossible technical question.

---

### Chapter 6: Verifying Claims & Authority
**6.1 Objective**
Audit the claims made by an individual about their own expertise.

**6.2 Cross-Referencing**
Compare their LinkedIn claims against actual project repositories or publication records.

**6.3 Third-Party Validation**
Find articles or news stories where they are quoted as an "Expert."

**6.4 Identifying "Resume Padding"**
Look for vague descriptions that might hide a lack of actual hands-on experience.

**6.5 Expected Outcome**
A "Credibility Report" that confirms or challenges the person's public image.

---

### Chapter 7: Research Synthesis & Reporting
**7.1 Objective**
Summarize the research into a concise, actionable brief.

**7.2 The "Introductory Brief"**
A 3-sentence summary of who they are and why they are relevant to you.

**7.3 Notable Achievements**
A list of their top 3 most significant contributions or career milestones.

**7.4 "How to Engage"**
Suggestions on the best way to approach or learn from this specific person.

**7.5 Pro-Tip**
Use this to build a "Speakers List" for a conference or a "Consultants List" for a project.

---

### Chapter 8: Command Reference & FAQ
**8.1 Objective**
Quick lookup for people research triggers.

**8.2 Core Tools**
*   `people_search_exa`, `web_search_advanced_personal_site`, `web_search_advanced_tweet`.

**8.3 FAQ: Is this illegal?**
No, it uses publicly available information. Always adhere to your local privacy laws.

**8.4 FAQ: What if I find nothing?**
They may have a high privacy setting or a common name. Use keywords like "Company" or "Location."

**8.5 Final Pro-Tip**
Researching the *person* behind the code is often just as important as researching the code itself.
